Pounded Yam and Egusi Soup

Nigerian Pounded Yam and Egusi Soup

This traditional Nigerian dish combines the creamy texture of pounded yam with the rich, nutty flavor of egusi soup. The egusi soup is made from ground melon seeds, vegetables, and a blend of spices, creating a hearty and satisfying meal that's perfect for special occasions or family gatherings.

Ingredients

  • Pounded yam (mashed yam)
  • Egusi seeds (ground melon seeds)
  • Onions
  • Garlic cloves
  • Tomatoes
  • Vegetable oil or palm oil
  • Seasoning cubes or salt
  • Water or stock
  • Fish or meat (optional)
  • Bell peppers (optional)

Steps

  1. Pound the yam: Boil the yam until tender, then pound it in a mortar and pestle until smooth and creamy.
  2. Prepare the egusi soup: Heat oil in a pot over medium heat. Add onions and garlic, saut√© until fragrant. Add tomatoes and bell peppers (if using), cook for 5 minutes.
  3. Add egusi seeds: Stir in the ground egusi seeds, cooking for 3-5 minutes to release their flavors.
  4. Add liquid: Pour in water or stock, bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low and simmer for 30 minutes.
  5. Add seasoning: Season with salt or seasoning cubes to taste.
  6. Serve: Serve the pounded yam alongside the egusi soup in a bowl. Garnish with fresh herbs if desired.
