Classic Spaghetti Bolognese

This classic spaghetti bolognese is a definition of a comfort and pleasing food. It features a rich, slow-simmered meat sauce (ragù) made with savory ground beef, a fragrant base of onion, carrort, and a robust tomato flavor. The sauce is hearty and thick, clinging to the long strands of spaghetti, and added with fresh basil and a generous dusting of Parmesan cheese, just like the image above.

Ingredients

  • Spaghetti pasta
  • Ground beef
  • Olive oil
  • Onion, finely chopped
  • Carrot, finely diced
  • Garlic cloves, minced
  • Canned Crush or Whole tomatoes
  • Tomato paste
  • Fresh basil leaves
  • Grated Parmesan cheese

Steps

  1. Prepare the vegetable:Heat olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and carrot. Sauté for about 5-7 minutes until softened.
  2. Cook the meat: Increase heat to medium-high. Add the ground beef and cook, breaking it apart with spoon, until it is fully browned and no longer pink.
  3. Add flavor base: Stir in the minced garlic and tomato paste, cooking for 1-2 minutes until fragrant and slightly darker in color.
  4. Simmer the sauce: Add the canned crush tomatoes, breaking them up with a spoon if using whole tomatoes. Season with salt and pepper. Reduce heat to low and let the sauce simmer gently for at least 30 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  5. Cook the pasta: About 10 minutes before the sauce is ready, cook the spaghetti according to package directions in a separate pot of salted boiling water until al dente (firm to the bite).
  6. Serve: Drain the pasta and serve immediately with a generous amount of sauce on top. Garnish with grated Parmesan Cheese and fresh basil leaves
